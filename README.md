## Introduction

This repository contains my complete Digital Literacy project submitted as part of the CSE0001 coursework at VIT Bhopal University. The project was designed to help students understand how digital tools are used responsibly in academic, professional, and everyday contexts.

While working on this project, I realized that digital literacy is not only about using technology but also about knowing how to communicate properly, build a professional online identity, verify information, and stay safe in digital environments. Each task in this repository represents a practical activity that helped me explore a different aspect of digital responsibility and digital communication.

All files are arranged task-wise, with supporting screenshots, documents, and written reflections included according to the project requirements.

---

## Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

In the first task, I created an infographic to explain the importance of digital literacy for college students. The infographic covers key ideas such as safe internet practices, password awareness, useful digital tools for academic work, and maintaining a responsible online presence.

The main purpose of this task was to present important digital concepts in a simple visual format so that they can be understood quickly and clearly. While designing it, I also understood how visual communication can make information more effective.

---

## Task 2 – Digital Portfolio Setup

For Task 2, I created and updated profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle to begin building a basic digital portfolio. I also created a GitHub profile README repository using my username, where I introduced myself, my academic background, and my learning goals.

This task helped me understand that creating a professional online identity should begin early, even during the first year of college, because these platforms gradually become important for projects, networking, and future opportunities.

---

## Task 3 – Digital Platform Exploration

In Task 3, I worked with Google Forms and created a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz containing five questions related to online safety, digital tools, and responsible digital practices. The form was tested by submitting one response and linking it to a spreadsheet to observe how responses are collected automatically.

I also explored HackerRank to understand how coding platforms work and how they are used to practice problem-solving and basic programming skills.

This task showed me how digital platforms can be used not only for learning but also for organizing information efficiently.

---

## Task 4 – Email Etiquette and Responsible Communication

Task 4 focused on professional digital communication. I drafted two formal emails: one requesting an assignment deadline extension from a professor, and another expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity.

I also prepared a social media checklist containing responsible online practices and common mistakes students should avoid while posting or communicating digitally.

This task made me more aware of how important tone, clarity, and structure are when writing emails in academic and professional situations.

---

## Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness and Prevention

For Task 5, I selected phishing as the cybercrime topic and prepared a case study explaining how phishing attacks usually happen, why students are often targeted, and what consequences such attacks can have.

Along with the case study, I created a prevention checklist covering practical online safety habits such as checking suspicious links, protecting OTPs, verifying UPI payments, and reporting fraud through official cybercrime channels.

This task helped me understand that online safety depends on small but careful habits followed consistently.

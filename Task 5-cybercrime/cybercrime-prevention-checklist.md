# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

1. Always read messages carefully before reacting, especially if they create urgency or ask for immediate action. Fraudulent emails and SMS messages often use fear or pressure to make people respond without thinking.

2. Before clicking any link, check whether the sender is genuine and whether the website address looks official. Even a small spelling difference in a link can indicate a fake website.

3. Never share OTPs, passwords, ATM PINs, or confidential banking details with anyone, even if the caller or sender claims to be from a bank, payment service, or trusted organization.

4. Use strong passwords for all important accounts and avoid keeping the same password for multiple platforms, because one leaked password can affect many accounts.

5. Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible, especially for email accounts, banking apps, and social media, as it adds extra security even if someone learns your password.

6. While making UPI payments, always verify the receiver’s name carefully before approving the transaction, because mistakes or fake payment requests can lead to direct financial loss.

7. Avoid scanning QR codes sent by unknown people or accepting payment requests from unfamiliar sources, particularly during online purchases, as scammers often misuse these methods.

8. If you notice suspicious transactions, account misuse, or possible cyber fraud, report it immediately through cybercrime.gov.in or contact the cybercrime helpline 1930 so that action can be taken quickly.

# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a common cybercrime in which attackers attempt to steal sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, bank details, 
or account credentials by pretending to be a trusted person or organization. 
These attacks usually happen through fake emails, SMS messages, phone calls, or websites
that are designed to look genuine so that the victim does not easily suspect fraud.

A realistic example would be a college student receiving an SMS that appears to come from a bank, 
warning that the account will be temporarily blocked unless identity verification is completed immediately. 
The message creates urgency and includes a link that opens a fake banking website. 
Since the page looks almost identical to the original website, the student enters login credentials, card details, and the OTP received on the phone. 
Within minutes, unauthorized money transfers take place from the account.

Phishing generally follows a simple pattern. 
First, the attacker sends a message that creates fear, urgency, or trust. 
Second, the victim clicks the provided link or responds to the message. 
Third, confidential details are entered without careful verification. 
Finally, the attacker uses this information for financial fraud, account misuse, or identity theft.

Students are often targeted because they frequently use UPI payments, online banking, email accounts, and digital platforms for academic work. 
The consequences can include financial loss, misuse of personal identity, and emotional stress. 
This shows why checking links, sender details, and website authenticity is extremely important before sharing any personal information online.
